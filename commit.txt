kernel_3d_visualizer: the dataflow edges — the CLINK–IMSCRIB–IFIX workaround helix.

The figure showed the stations and the winding but not the flows between them.
Now the edges are drawn, derived from kernel.rs tick semantics rather than
hand-placed:

- The workaround helix (yellow): CLINK → ⊙ rides the (1,1) winding backward
  into the pinch; IFIX → ⊙ rides it forward the long way round, so the two
  flows arrive at the identity from OPPOSITE directions and thread ⊙ as a
  helix. The seal-side CLINK₂ gets its own thinner strand forward through the
  seal. Each strand sits at its own lift off the gold tube so the braid reads
  as a braid, not a sheath (the first pass sheathed the whole loop — fixed).
- The empty chord (dotted white): the direct CLINK ↔ IFIX connection along
  the winding through FFUSE exists in the SEQUENCE but carries no dataflow —
  the workaround IS that the two work opcodes compose only through the
  identity at the pinch. Drawn dotted, labeled.
- The R0 bus (blue dashed): the one true register edge set in agent_loop —
  AREV writes R0, IFIX and TANCH read it as the address. Bulged chords with
  arrowheads, distinct from sequence flow. (CLINK's R1/R2 reads and R3 write
  and IMSCRIB's R4–R7 writes have no partner in the loop: no edge invented
  for them.) Stack dataflow needs no edges: it IS the gold winding.

Same idiom as the hopfionic figure's dataflow block (solidTube / dashedEdge /
arrowHead / edgeLabel ported), so the two figures speak one language. Legend
keys the three edge colors.
